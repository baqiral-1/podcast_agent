Freedom at Midnight

The final months of the Raj unfold as rival visions, hurried borders, and exhausted officials race toward independence. Four episodes track the political bargaining, human catastrophe, and the assassination that defined a nation’s birth.

Episode 1

The Raj Unravels

An exhausted empire and irreconcilable demands push India toward Partition.

Britain's war-drained empire reaches the end of its tether. London's Cabinet dithers, Congress and the Muslim League harden their demands, and communal violence flares in Bengal. Gandhi walks through Noakhali, insisting that independence means moral repair as well as political transfer. Into this storm steps Louis Mountbatten, tasked with engineering a handover that the Raj no longer has the legitimacy or manpower to control. The episode tracks how deadlock gives way to urgency: why a united India starts to look impossible, how the timetable accelerates, and how Partition becomes thinkable, then inevitable. It is the story of an empire that can no longer rule, leaders who can no longer agree, and a clock that begins to run faster than any of them expect.

Episode 2

Drawing the Lines of Freedom

Boundaries are drawn in haste, and secrecy becomes a strategy.

Independence now has a date, but its shape is still contested. The princes bargain for terms, administrators weigh how to divide armies, assets, and civil services, and the Radcliffe boundary commission is asked to draw lines on a map it barely knows. Mountbatten's plan requires speed, and speed means secrecy: the borders will be announced only after power has already been transferred. The episode follows the scramble to define Pakistan, the negotiations over Punjab and Bengal, and the mounting fear that the act of drawing lines will ignite violence. Behind the scenes, officials attempt to choreograph a peaceful handover while communities arm themselves. It is a story of statecraft under impossible constraints, when legal precision and human lives are both sacrificed to the race against chaos.

Episode 3

Partition Inferno

Partition unleashes mass violence and a refugee catastrophe across the new borders.

Partition arrives as a human catastrophe. Punjab fractures into massacre and counter-massacre, trains arrive packed with the dead, and refugee columns stretch across the new borders in both directions. The episode traces how the administrative machinery buckles: police desert, local leaders lose control, and rumors turn whole districts into killing grounds. In Bengal and Delhi the violence is different but no less terrifying, and women become targets of abduction and vengeance. Amid the chaos, the new governments struggle to protect their citizens while also trying to create themselves--issuing orders, moving troops, and establishing authority in cities that are burning. The story is not just one of slaughter, but of the birth of two states in the middle of it, and of the moral and political price that independence exacted.

Episode 4

The Death of the Mahatma

Gandhi's final campaign meets a hardening extremist conspiracy.

With independence achieved, Gandhi turns to the task he believes still unfinished: saving India's moral center. He fasts to halt riots, presses leaders to protect minorities, and walks through neighborhoods where fear still rules. His insistence that Pakistan be paid its promised funds inflames Hindu extremists who see him as betraying the nation. The episode follows the tightening conspiracy, the atmosphere of Delhi in early 1948, and Gandhi's final public appearances, where he pleads for reconciliation even as threats mount around him. The assassination is sudden, but the build-up is long: a clash between pluralism and ethno-religious nationalism. The episode closes on the shockwave his death sends through India and Pakistan, and the brief, fragile unity it forces into being.
