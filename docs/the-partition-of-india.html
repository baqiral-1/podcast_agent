The Partition of India

Eight episodes. Nine books.

The series begins by asking whether partition was a failure of individuals or of structures, then moves through the specific mechanisms — colonial engineering, electoral mobilization, the politics of speed, the drawing of borders, the organization of violence — showing how each mechanism transformed contingency into catastrophe.

The middle episodes reveal the hidden gears: personal relationships that functioned as constitutional mechanisms, a prophet whose moral authority grew as his political power waned, and a third force (the Sikhs) that nobody modeled.

The final episodes trace how partition’s logic reproduced itself — in the princely states, in Kashmir, in the constitutions of both nations, in memory and amnesia — converging on the insight that partition did not resolve the communal question but merely relocated and perpetuated it. The series closes by asking whether the furies unleashed in 1947 can ever be contained.

Drawn from 9 works including Freedom at Midnight, India After Gandhi, Midnight’s Furies, The Great Partition, Indian Summer, Jinnah of Pakistan, and others.

This episode establishes the deep structural foundations of partition by tracing how British colonial policies transformed fluid religious identities into rigid political categories. This episode examines the Cabinet Mission of 1946 as the last serious alternative to partition — a three-tier federal scheme that came tantalizingly close to preserving a united India.
