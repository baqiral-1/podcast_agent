India After Gandhi

Stories and analyses adapted from Ramachandra Guha's history of modern India.

Episode 1

Nation, Partition, and the Making of the Republic

In this episode, we trace the dramatic and often painful formation of modern India, from the idea of the country as an unlikely and deeply divided civilization to the political struggle that brought independence.

We follow the collapse of empire, the trauma of Partition, Gandhi's final efforts to contain communal bloodshed, and Nehru's attempt to define India as a secular democratic state. Along the way, the story explores the arguments for and against division, the human cost of freedom, the pressures of refugee resettlement, and the enormous challenge of holding together a nation many believed could never endure.

Episode 2

Democracy, Development, and the Fractures of a New Nation

In this episode, we follow India through one of the most consequential stretches of its early post-independence history, as the new republic struggles to hold together political authority, democratic ambition, and regional diversity.

The story moves from Congress infighting and the gamble of the first general election to Cold War pressures abroad, economic modernization at home, linguistic conflict, and the uneasy integration of Kashmir and tribal borderlands. Along the way, it shows how Nehru's India was built not through consensus alone, but through argument, improvisation, and repeated tests of the state's legitimacy. It is a portrait of a nation trying to define itself under extraordinary strain.

Episode 3

Power, Nationhood, and the Strains of Democracy

We follow India through a decisive phase of its early republic, when independence gave way to the harder work of governing a vast and divided nation.

The story moves from Congress infighting and the first democratic election to Cold War diplomacy, state-led development, linguistic conflict, the Kashmir dispute, and unrest in India's tribal borderlands. At the center is Jawaharlal Nehru's effort to hold together democracy, national unity, and social transformation under immense pressure. What emerges is not a smooth nation-building story, but a portrait of a state repeatedly tested by regional demands, ideological rivalry, and the challenge of turning freedom into a durable political order.

Episode 4

From Succession to Upheaval: India's Long Political Reckoning

We trace India's turbulent political journey from the aftermath of Nehru's death to the unstable close of the 1980s.

The story moves through war, succession struggles, Indira Gandhi's rise, her populist reinvention of Congress, and the mounting pressures that reshaped Indian democracy. Along the way, we follow regional unrest, opposition coalitions, the strains on institutions, and the changing relationship between national power and popular anger. By the time Rajiv Gandhi enters center stage, the old order is visibly fraying. What emerges is a portrait of a democracy under pressure, improvising through crisis, ambition, violence, and reinvention.

Episode 5

Mandal, Markets, and the Movies: India's Next Great Transformation

This episode follows India as politics spills into every part of public life.

We move through the Mandal moment and the reshaping of caste politics, the rise of new parties and social coalitions, and the unrest that exposed how fragile state power could be. From Bihar's breakdowns to militancy in Kashmir, the story shows a democracy under strain but constantly reinventing itself. It also tracks another revolution unfolding in parallel: the transformation of culture, entertainment, and national aspiration through film, music, and cricket. The result is a portrait of modern India at a turning point, where identity, power, and popular culture begin to define the country's next era.
