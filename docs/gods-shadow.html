God's Shadow

In the fifteenth century, one empire held the world in its grip. From the Black Sea frontier to the gates of Europe, Ottoman sultans waged wars that shaped the destiny of three continents. A ruthless prince, overlooked and exiled to a distant province, would eliminate his own brothers, seize the throne, and launch campaigns so devastating they redrew the map of the known world. But the Ottomans' shadow stretched even further than their armies -- into the voyages of Columbus and the very discovery of the Americas. This is the story of how Islam's mightiest dynasty secretly shaped the modern world.

Episode 1

Origins: The Ottoman World and Selim's Rise

When Sultan Mehmet dies, a deadly succession crisis erupts in the Ottoman Empire. One brother races to seize the throne while the other flees into the arms of the Pope himself. But it is a young prince, banished to the remote Black Sea frontier, who will prove the most dangerous of all. From the city of Amasya to the volatile borderlands of Trabzon, Selim forges alliances, crushes enemies, and prepares for a confrontation that will shake the empire to its foundations. The game of thrones has only just begun.

The Ottoman Empire stands at a crossroads. Sultan Mehmet the Conqueror is dead, and his sons are locked in a brutal race for the throne. Bayezit moves first, rushing to claim power in a succession crisis that will echo across decades. His brother Cem, outmaneuvered and desperate, flees westward -- eventually becoming a pawn of European courts and the Vatican itself, meeting Pope Innocent VIII in Rome as diplomacy and holy war intertwine. But far from the imperial capital, a third figure watches and waits. Young Selim, dispatched to govern the distant frontier province of Trabzon on the Black Sea coast, is building something far more dangerous than a provincial administration. Together with his mother Gulbahar, he constructs a power base, forges military alliances, and tests himself in battle against the rising Safavid threat along the empire's most volatile border. As the episode traces the Ottoman world from its origins through the politics of succession, a pattern emerges: the empire's future will not belong to the cautious or the diplomatic. It will belong to the ruthless. And Selim is only getting started.

Episode 2

Columbus, Islam, and Selim's Seizure of Power

Christopher Columbus sails west -- not for spices or glory, but to outflank the Ottoman Empire in a centuries-old holy war. Meanwhile, Selim defies his own father, refuses to surrender conquered territory, and ignites a succession crisis that will leave bodies in its wake. He spends a pivotal summer in Crimea marshaling forces, then marches on Istanbul itself. One by one, his half-brothers fall. Ahmed is cornered and humiliated. Korkud is eliminated. By the end, only one prince remains standing -- and the Ottoman Empire will never be the same.

The voyages of Christopher Columbus were never simply about discovery. They were born from centuries of crusade against Islam, driven by the terrifying reality of Ottoman dominance over the trade routes and strategic chokepoints of the known world. As European powers scrambled to find alternatives to Ottoman-controlled passages, Columbus sailed west into the unknown -- and the consequences would reshape both hemispheres forever. But the real drama unfolds in the Ottoman heartland. Selim, no longer content to govern a frontier province, openly defies Sultan Bayezit's orders and refuses to return territories he has seized. The empire fractures as three princes -- Selim, Ahmed, and Korkud -- maneuver for supremacy. Selim retreats to Crimea for a fateful summer of alliance-building, then launches his march southward toward Istanbul. The succession war that follows is savage and personal. Selim corners his half-brother Ahmed in a prison cell, hurling insults before sealing his fate. With his rivals eliminated through a combination of military force and ruthless calculation, Selim declares himself the sole sultan. The stage is now set for campaigns that will reshape the entire Muslim world.

Episode 3

Sultan of the World: Conquests, Death, and Legacy

Selim now holds the Ottoman throne -- and he intends to use it. In a series of devastating campaigns, he turns his armies against the Safavids and the Mamluks, conquering vast territories and shattering the old balance of power across the Islamic world. Ancient capitals fall. Empires crumble. The Ottoman realm expands to a scale that dwarfs anything before it. But even as Selim remakes the map, a question looms: what happens when the most dangerous sultan in Ottoman history meets his own mortality?

With his brothers dead and the throne secured through blood, Selim unleashes the full power of the Ottoman war machine. His first target: the Safavid Empire to the east, whose Shia ideology and expanding influence represent both a theological and military threat. The campaign is devastating, and the Safavid forces are shattered in a confrontation that redraws the boundaries of the Islamic world. But Selim is far from finished. He turns south toward the Mamluk Sultanate, the ancient power that has controlled Egypt, Syria, and the holy cities of Mecca and Medina for centuries. In a series of rapid conquests, the Mamluks collapse, and Selim absorbs their territories into an Ottoman domain of staggering size. He now presides over an empire stretching from the Balkans to North Africa, from the Persian frontier to the Arabian Peninsula. The balance of power across three continents has been permanently altered. Yet Selim's reign proves shockingly brief. The sultan who conquered more territory than almost any ruler in history faces the one enemy he cannot defeat. His legacy, however, will outlast him -- shaping not only his grandson Suleiman's golden age, but the trajectory of the modern world itself.
