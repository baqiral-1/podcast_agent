The Last Mughal

A narrative history collection tracing the collapse of Mughal Delhi, the uprising of 1857, and the violent remaking of empire.

Episode 1

Empire in Decline: Delhi Before the Storm

This episode sets the stage for the fall of the Mughal world by tracing the atmosphere of late Mughal Delhi before the uprising of 1857.

We move from the death and memory of Bahadur Shah Zafar to the cultural brilliance of his court, the city's layered social life, and the growing distance between British rulers and the society they claimed to govern. The story follows the collapse of older forms of coexistence, the rise of evangelical arrogance, the recovery of Indian voices through the Mutiny Papers, and the tensions building inside the Bengal Army. What emerges is a portrait of a city rich in culture, yet already living under the shadow of catastrophe.

Episode 2

Delhi in Revolt: Mutiny, Siege, and Urban Ruin

This episode follows the uprising as it erupts into open revolt and turns Delhi into the center of a brutal imperial struggle.

We trace the arrival of the Meerut sepoys, the panic and killings that reshape the city, the uneasy role of Bahadur Shah Zafar, and the increasingly chaotic alliance between rebels, courtiers, clerics, and townspeople. As British forces close in, the story moves through plunder, religious tension, hunger, factionalism, and siege warfare, showing how hopes of restoration gave way to fear and fragmentation. By the time the assault on Delhi reaches its climax, the city has become both a symbolic capital of resistance and a shattered landscape of violence.

Episode 3

After Delhi: Trial, Exile, and the End of the Mughals

This episode traces the aftermath of the uprising, from the British reconquest of Delhi to the exile and death of the last Mughal emperor.

We follow the humiliation of Zafar, the killing of the princes, the desolation of the city, and the trial that recast a ruined court as the center of a supposed conspiracy. The story then turns to Rangoon, where exile reduces the imperial household to obscurity, and finally to the longer consequences of the Mughal collapse for Indian politics, memory, and Muslim identity. It is a story not just about the fall of a dynasty, but about how an old civilization was broken and reinterpreted under empire.
