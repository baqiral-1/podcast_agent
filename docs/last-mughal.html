The Last Mughal

A three-part portrait of Delhi before, during, and after the 1857 rebellion. Courtly life and cultural brilliance give way to siege, retribution, and exile as the last Mughal dynasty collapses.

Episode 1

The World Before the Siege

Delhi's courtly world glitters even as rebellion gathers in the shadows.

Delhi in the 1850s is a city of poetry, markets, and overlapping faiths, but also of British ambition and a court in slow decline. We meet Bahadur Shah Zafar, an emperor with little power and a palace full of poets, princes, and rival factions. The episode walks the rhythms of the city, from the Red Fort to the cantonment, and shows how older accommodations between Hindus and Muslims begin to fracture under evangelical confidence and imperial reform. A wedding procession tries to restage imperial splendor while the machinery of British rule tightens around it. Outside the palace walls, new religious currents, rumors, and resentments build a second Delhi alongside the first. This is a portrait of a capital that still believes in its own grandeur, even as it senses the end. Both courts listen for a storm. By the time the city wakes to another ordinary morning, the tinder for rebellion is already in place.

Episode 2

Outbreak and Stalemate

The siege drags on as rival leaders and British forces grind a city to exhaustion.

The mutiny reaches Delhi and everything stalls between fury and hesitation. British forces regroup on the Ridge while the city erupts, and Bahadur Shah becomes a reluctant symbol for a rebellion he cannot command. Princes rush to court, militias form, and Delhi turns into a city without a single authority. Outside the walls, Hodson and the British advance; inside, Mirza Mughal and Bakht Khan struggle to coordinate defense and politics. The besiegers become besieged as monsoon, disease, and exhaustion grind both camps. Reinforcements arrive, but unity does not. The episode follows the long, brutal summer of 1857 when victory is always promised and rarely delivered. Delhi is shelled, alliances wobble, and a decisive assault is delayed again and again. By the time the British are ready to strike, the city is starving, and the court is divided against itself. The question is not whether the siege will break, but how much will survive it.

Episode 3

Fall of Delhi, Death of a Dynasty

The fall of Delhi ends a dynasty and leaves a city haunted by loss.

The British finally storm Delhi, and the city becomes a site of retribution. Princes are executed at the Bloody Gate, neighborhoods are looted, and the emperor's court is caged and starved. Bahadur Shah Zafar is put on trial, reduced to a powerless defendant in a case built around conspiracy and vengeance. As British authority returns, the old capital is emptied and remade, while poets like Ghalib walk through ruins that no longer feel like home. The episode follows the fall of a dynasty: judgment, exile, and a long road to Rangoon. There, the last Mughal dies in a wooden prison, and Delhi is left to remember him in fragments. This is not just the end of a revolt, but the end of an imperial world. By the time the empire looks stable again, it has erased the city it claimed to save. What remains is a memory, and a question about who gets to write it.
