The Last Mughal

A narrative history collection tracing the collapse of Mughal Delhi, the uprising of 1857, and the violent remaking of empire.

Episode 1

The Last Mughal World Before the Storm

Delhi in the 1840s and 1850s still glows with poetry, court ritual, and the fragile charisma of Bahadur Shah Zafar, even as British power narrows the space around him. We follow Zinat Mahal’s succession ambitions, rivalries among princes and courtiers, and a city balanced between Mughal legitimacy and an expanding empire.

A wedding procession in the Red Fort and a court still performing itself mask a thinning sovereignty. Missionary pressure, annexations, and daily humiliations harden attitudes, while sepoys face blocked promotions, pay cuts, and rumors around the Enfield cartridges. Through Indian records and lived detail, the episode rebuilds a composite urban world and shows how confidence erodes. By spring 1857, warnings multiply and the ground begins to shift.

Episode 2

Delhi in Revolt and Under Siege

May 1857 detonates as the Meerut mutiny spills into Delhi, British authority collapses, and Bahadur Shah Zafar is pressed into symbolic leadership. Inside the restored court, Mirza Mughal and rival factions struggle to impose order while refugees flood the streets and rumors race ahead of fact.

Outside the walls, the British regroup on the Ridge, build intelligence networks, and tighten the siege. Hunger rises, morale frays, and violence multiplies as hope collides with poor coordination and divided aims. The episode tracks telegram delays, escapes, and desperate governance as the city becomes a battlefield. Reinforcements and Nicholson’s arrival tip the balance toward assault.

Episode 3

Conquest, Retribution, and the End of the Mughals

The British storm Delhi and unleash a campaign of retribution: executions, looting, and scorched neighborhoods that shatter the city’s social fabric. Zafar is captured at Humayun’s Tomb, his sons are killed, and the surviving court is hunted down or forced into exile.

We follow the trials that recast the uprising as conspiracy, the seizure of property, and the slow dismantling of Delhi’s composite culture. British officers debate vengeance and legality as the ruined city becomes a test case for empire. Refugees scatter, loyalists are punished, and the emperor is sent to Rangoon, where his grave is kept unmarked. The episode closes on how violence and memory reshaped the end of Mughal sovereignty.
